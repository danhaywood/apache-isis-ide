3. Create a new Property, Collection or Action

The NO IDE provides a collection of templates to create properties, collections and actions. Navigate within the class definition (but outside a method definition) and press ctrl+space: this will list all templates available. If you press ctrl+space 2 times further you will see just the templates applicable to Naked Objects Domain Objects (hence it's easiest to hit ctrl+space 3 times and go straight to this list):

There are 3 variants of the templates for each of properties, collections and actions, following the format noXY, where:

  • X = a (action), c (collection), p (property)

  • Y = c (common), f (full), s (supporting)

Applying the common template (eg nopc) followed by the supporting template (eg nops) is equivalent to applying the full template (eg nopf). The templates are split this way because in practice most class members don't need the whole gamut of supporting methods (or at least, they are only required later on as additional business rules are uncovered; while initially coming to grips with a domain object model they tend to get in the way).
